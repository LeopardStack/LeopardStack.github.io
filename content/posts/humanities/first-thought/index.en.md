People who write code tend to live inside a world of certainty.

The compiler never equivocates: right is right, wrong is wrong. Over time we
grow used to treating every problem as an engineering problem — find the
optimal solution, close the ticket.

But life refuses to work that way. Reading a novel, watching a public debate
unfold, spending an ordinary evening with family — these things have no
optimal solution, sometimes no answer at all. They demand a different
capacity: **to linger in ambiguity, and to think amid uncertainty**.

So this blog has two rooms:

- One holds **tech notes**: Java, C++, Python, algorithms, system design —
  knowledge that is definite and verifiable;
- The other holds **essays**: reading notes, reflections, observations on
  society — questions that stay blurry and deserve to be chewed over again
  and again.

They may seem unrelated, but they serve the same purpose —

> To turn vague feelings into clear words.

Writing is slow thinking. May every post here, whether it speaks of code or
of people, still hold up when my future self comes back to read it.
